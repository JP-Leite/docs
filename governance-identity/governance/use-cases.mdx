Hyland Governance provides a solution for legal compliance management, risk mitigation, efficient data management, data security and privacy, operational continuity, audits and accountability, and data hoarding prevention.

See below a list of examples where using Hyland Governance can contribute positively to data handling:

Examples

-
An HR department creates and manages personnel files for each employee, including onboarding documents, contracts, performance reviews, and termination paperwork.

The HR department sets the retention policy to retain records for five years after an employee leaves and then automatically archives them. Once the retention period expires, the records are securely deleted to reduce storage costs and maintain compliance with labour laws.

-
A consulting firm maintains extensive records of client projects, including contracts, project deliverables, meeting notes, and communications.

Per policy, records are retained for seven years after project completion for reference or legal purposes and then automatically archived. After the retention period, archived records are securely destroyed to eliminate outdated information and reduce storage costs.

-
A company's finance department uses a secure digital system to manage records such as invoices, receipts, tax documents, and financial statements.

Per policy, these records are retained for a minimum of ten years to comply with IRS and tax audit requirements and then securely deleted. This retention policy ensures that the company retains necessary documents for tax and audit purposes but discards them when they are no longer needed to minimize data security risks.

-
A law firm stores legal documents for each case they manage, including client communications, case files, discovery documents, and court filings.

According to policy, case files are retained for ten years after case closure or as required by jurisdictional requirements and then archived. After the retention period, case files are archived to make room for new cases and later securely deleted, which is in line with legal and regulatory guidelines.
