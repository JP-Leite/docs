## How Auto Ingestion Works

Once the Confluence Connector is configured, **Auto Ingestion** monitors your selected Confluence site for changes. Whenever a page or blog post is created, edited, or deleted, the connector automatically pushes the updated content and its metadata to Hyland Content Intelligence.

This ensures that AI agents querying your knowledge base always have access to the most current version of your documentation — without any manual effort.

<Info>
  Auto Ingestion is triggered by **content save events** in Confluence. Minor edits and draft saves may not trigger ingestion depending on your Confluence version and configuration.
</Info>

---

## Enabling Auto Ingestion

Auto Ingestion is enabled as part of the standard connector configuration. No additional steps are required beyond completing the [Ingestion Configuration](/integrations/confluence/ingestion-configuration) and [API Token Configuration](/integrations/confluence/atlassian-api-token-configuration).

Once credentials are saved and validated, the connector begins listening for events on the configured Confluence site automatically.

## What Gets Ingested

| Content Type | Ingested? |
| --- | --- |
| Pages | ✅ Yes |
| Blog Posts | ✅ Yes |
| Attachments | ❌ Not supported |
| Comments | ❌ Not supported |
| Jira Links | ❌ Not supported |

## Auto Ingestion vs. Bulk Ingestion

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Auto Ingestion" icon="rotate">
    **Best for:** Ongoing sync after initial setup.

    Automatically captures changes as they happen. Requires no user action. Ideal for keeping CIC up to date with daily editorial activity.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Bulk Ingestion" icon="bolt">
    **Best for:** Initial data load or backfill.

    Manually select and push a batch of pages to CIC in one operation. Use this when onboarding a new Confluence space or recovering from an outage.

    [Learn more →](/integrations/confluence/bulk-ingestion)
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<Accordion title="Changes in Confluence are not appearing in Content Intelligence">
  1. Confirm the connector is fully configured and credentials are valid — go to **Apps → Hyland Content Intelligence → Settings**.
  2. Check that the affected page is in a Confluence **space** that is in scope for the configured Source ID.
  3. Wait a few minutes — there may be a brief processing delay before changes propagate to CIC.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="A deleted page is still appearing in search results">
  Deleted content is eventually de-indexed during the next sync cycle. If it persists, trigger a **Bulk Ingestion** on the affected space to force a refresh.
</Accordion>
